01 The world & your mission

2157 CE — three decades after the Collapse.

A quantum collapse detonated without warning and shattered the megacity of NEXUM into 40,000 drifting fragments. From the wreckage rose three factions — the Architects, the Wardens, and the Ghosts — each chasing the same prize: Fragment Zero, the last intact shard, rumoured to hold the key to reversing the Collapse.

You arrive on Fragment Zero as the Void closes in. Hostile Void Crawlers surge from the dark perimeter in escalating waves. Your job is simple to say and hard to do: build defensible cover, hold your ground, and push back the Void through five rising threat levels.

Core promise: TacticalCraft is the rare game where building and shooting live in the same minute. You will break terrain for materials, raise walls for cover, then turn and fight — over and over, faster each level.

The story unfolds as you play

Each threat level opens with a story chapter — the narrative is told through the fight, not just in the intro. Across five chapters you uncover what Fragment Zero really is, why the Void wants it, and what holding it means for the ruins of NEXUM.

CHAPTER I · Level 1

Arrival

You land on the last intact shard. The Void is already testing the edges.

CHAPTER II · Level 2

The Signal

Something beneath the fragment is calling the Crawlers. NEXUM was hiding something here.

CHAPTER III · Level 3

Adaptation

The enemy learns. A faction warns you: you're not defending rubble — you're defending the wound.

CHAPTER IV · Level 4

The Truth

Fragment Zero is the seed of the Collapse — and the key to reversing it. Everyone is converging.

CHAPTER V · Level 5

Tremor

The fragment fights back as reality frays. Hold the core through the final stand — and glimpse what waits below in The Deep Fragment.

02 Getting started

  1. Watch (or Skip) the cinematic intro.
  2. On the menu, choose your class — the 3D preview shows your operative. Each class changes how you play (see §4).
  3. Type a callsign (your name) in the field.
  4. Pick a mode:
    • ⚡ Play Online — join the live server and fight alongside / against real players.
    • 🎮 Solo Mode — practice with 3 AI squadmates and the full Void Crawler threat.
  5. Click to lock your mouse and take control. Press Esc any time to release it.
New here? Start in Solo Mode as the Builder. It's the most forgiving way to learn the build-then-fight rhythm before going online.

06 Threat levels & how to clear them

Fragment Zero escalates through five threat levels. Each level raises Void Crawler count, speed, and aggression. Clear a level by surviving its wave window and reaching the kill quota — then the next level begins automatically.

1

Perimeter Probe

A handful of Void Crawlers test your defences. Slow, few, predictable. This is your setup window.
CLEAR: Survive 90s & destroy 5 Crawlers → fortify a basic wall while you fight.
2

Void Surge

Crawlers arrive in tighter groups and move faster. Gaps in your cover start to cost you.
CLEAR: Destroy 12 Crawlers → seal your fort to a single defensible entrance.
3

Crawler Mutation

Mutated Crawlers hit harder and swarm from two directions. An AI World Event fires — watch the banner.
CLEAR: Destroy 20 Crawlers & keep your core structure standing → use elevation.
4

Void Storm

Visibility drops and the surge is relentless. Repair under fire (Engineer shines). Mistakes compound fast.
CLEAR: Survive the storm window & destroy 30 Crawlers → rely on chokepoints, not open ground.
5

Fragment Zero Tremor

The fragment itself becomes unstable. Maximum Crawler density and speed. This is the proving ground.
CLEAR: Hold Fragment Zero to the end of the wave & destroy 40 Crawlers → victory screen + score.
How progression works: Your kill count (top HUD) and the AI World Event banners track the threat level. When you meet a level's quota and survive its window, the next wave escalates automatically — there is no menu break, the pressure just rises. Falling to 0 HP returns you to the last safe spawn; your built structures persist.
Score & moving on: Blocks Placed, Blocks Broken, and Kills are tracked live in the HUD. Clearing Level 5 is the current win condition; online, your placed-block and kill totals feed the live server leaderboard via /api/status.

07 Building & fortifying

Press F to enter Build mode (the mode badge turns purple). Right-click places the selected block on the face you're aiming at; left-click breaks. Switch blocks with 1–8 or the scroll wheel.

Block types

Grass Dirt Stone Sand Wood Metal Sandbag Brick

Metal and Brick are your toughest cover. Sandbag is the Builder's free, instant wall. Stack two blocks high to break enemy line-of-sight and force Crawlers around a chokepoint.

The golden loop: Break terrain → collect the material → wall off everything but one entrance → stand inside and fight through the gap. Repeat, reinforcing each level.

08 Combat & the Void Crawlers

Press F to return to Combat mode (badge turns red). Left-click attacks down your crosshair within your class reach. Void Crawlers — dark cubes with glowing red eyes — move straight toward you and deal contact damage, so never let them stack on you.

Each Crawler takes 3 hits to destroy. Destroyed Crawlers respawn from the perimeter after ~15 seconds, and the respawn rate climbs with the threat level. Your Kills counter (top HUD) is your progression meter.

Watch the red dots on the minimap (top-right). They are incoming Crawlers. If several converge from one side, reposition behind cover before they arrive.

09 Multiplayer

In Play Online, you share Fragment Zero with real players in real time. You'll see their nameplates, their builds appear in your world instantly, and the chat (T) is live. The online roster shows on the right of the HUD.

The world is shared: blocks you place or break sync to everyone, and the server keeps the recent world state so late-joiners see the current fort. Coordinate with your faction, divide build and defence duties, and push the threat levels together.

Connection note: The free server may sleep after inactivity and take ~30 seconds to wake on the first join. If "Server offline" shows, wait a moment and retry, or drop into Solo Mode.

10 Survival tips

Fortify first

Use Level 1's calm to wall up. A finished chokepoint before Level 2 is worth more than any number of early kills.

Hold high ground

The watchtowers and stacked blocks give you sightlines. Snipers especially should fight from elevation.

One entrance only

Crawlers funnel. Seal every gap but one and you turn a swarm into a single-file line you can clear.

Mind your back

From Level 3 they flank. Glance at the minimap between kills — red dots behind you are how runs end.

Repair, don't rebuild

Engineers can patch damaged cover mid-fight. Keep the core standing rather than starting over.

Play your class

Soldier tanks the door, Builder/Engineer maintain walls, Sniper picks from range, Medic keeps everyone up. Online, split the roles.
